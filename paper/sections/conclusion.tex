\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have introduced ECAG-Bench, a benchmark of 332 constructive problems in algebraic geometry designed to evaluate a mode of mathematical reasoning---object construction---that is central to mathematical practice but underrepresented in existing LLM benchmarks.
Our four-layer verification pipeline, anchored by CAS-based property verification, enables reliable evaluation of non-unique mathematical constructions.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
ECAG-Bench focuses on algebraic geometry, which limits the generalizability of our findings to other areas of mathematics.
The CAS verification layer, while powerful, cannot verify all properties---some require human judgment or more sophisticated formal methods.
Our current evaluation uses single-turn prompting; multi-turn interaction may significantly improve model performance.

\paragraph{Future work.}
We plan to:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item Expand the benchmark to cover additional areas (arithmetic geometry, algebraic topology, commutative algebra).
  \item Implement the full CAS verification pipeline with automated SageMath/Macaulay2 scripts.
  \item Evaluate models in multi-turn interactive settings, where they can refine constructions based on CAS feedback.
  \item Investigate whether fine-tuning on algebraic geometry textbooks improves constructive reasoning.
  \item Release a public leaderboard for tracking progress on ECAG-Bench.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Broader impact.}
Constructive mathematical reasoning is a prerequisite for using LLMs as research tools in mathematics.
If models cannot construct examples and counterexamples, they cannot assist mathematicians in the exploratory phase of research---the phase where intuition is built and conjectures are tested.
ECAG-Bench provides a concrete measure of progress toward this goal.
